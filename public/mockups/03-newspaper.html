Twenty-Eight Days in the Saddle Carry Sarah Johnson to Victory
as a Field of Twelve Records Their Strongest May to Date

The Month That Was

By our Cycling Correspondent

No sooner had the bells of May the First struck than the peloton of the May is Bike Month challenge departed in earnest, twelve riders strong, into a month that would prove neither merciful nor monotonous.

Mrs. Sarah Johnson of the sarah‑j‑cycling stable, having previously placed second in the year MMXXIII, opened her account with a brisk Wednesday commute and did not relent. By the second week, observers noted she had not missed a single day — a feat she would extend to twenty-eight by month’s end.

Her closest challenger, Mr. Marcus Chen, faltered but twice, and only by virtue of an obligation to a wedding in the city of Reno. The judges record his streak as twenty-seven.

On the Climbs

Of vertical metres there was no shortage. Mr. David Kim, ever the partisan of the gradient, accumulated 19,650 feet of elevation, surpassing all rivals and securing the polka-dotted laurels.
